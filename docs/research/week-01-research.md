Week 1 – Research & Discovery
Overview

This week was mostly about figuring out the project before actually building anything. I focused on understanding the idea better, tightening up the proposal, and making sure the project is worth moving forward with. The main goal was to clearly understand what the project is, who it’s for, and why it matters.

Proposal & Business Case

After reviewing the proposal materials, I went back and cleaned up my project idea. I made sure the purpose and end goal made sense and were easy to explain. This showed me how important it is to define things early so there’s less confusion later. Having a clear scope and plan helps keep the project from getting messy once development starts.

Discovery & Kickoff

The discovery phase helped me realize how important it is to slow down and ask the right questions before jumping into code. This includes thinking about user needs, project goals, and possible problems ahead of time. Doing this early helps make sure the project is actually solving the right problem.

Evaluating the Idea

The idea evaluation resources focused on making sure a project solves a real problem and not just something that sounds cool. This made me think more about whether my project is useful, how it compares to similar ideas, and what success would actually look like if it was developed further.

30-Second Pitch

Pitch:

This project is about building a portfolio-ready application that shows real development skills. It focuses on staying organized, writing clear documentation, and improving the project over time so it can be used as both a learning experience and a professional portfolio piece.

Reflection

Overall, this week showed me that planning and research really matter. Taking the time to define goals and expectations early makes the rest of the project smoother and helps avoid unnecessary rework later on.
